Bryan Robertson

Artist & Educator Dean of Visual and Performing Arts, Yavapai College

Newsreel

Highlights from Bryan Robertson's artistic career, exhibitions, publications, and professional achievements.

February 2024

Session Chair at CAA Conference

Bryan Robertson chaired the session "AI In the Studio Art Classroom" at the College Art Association Annual Conference in Chicago, continuing his exploration of AI's role in art education.

January 2024

Exhibition: Chromacosm: Our Universe in Color

Work selected for national juried exhibition at Schack Art Center in Everett, WA, showcasing Robertson's continued exploration of color theory and perception.

2024

Appointed Dean of Visual and Performing Arts

Bryan Robertson was appointed Dean of Visual and Performing Arts at Yavapai College in Prescott, Arizona, after serving as Visual Art Department Chair from 2021-2024.

September 2023

Publication: Creative Convergence

Co-authored "Creative Convergence, The AI Renaissance in Art and Design," published by Springer Nature, exploring artificial intelligence's impact on contemporary art practices.

August 2023

Research Publication on AI in Drawing Education

"A Matter of Perspective: A Case Study in the Use of AI-Generative Art in the Drawing Classroom" published in The International Journal of New Media, Technology and the Arts.

April 2023

Presentations at FATE Conference

Delivered two presentations at the FATE Conference at Rocky Mountain College of Art and Design: "Synesthesia in the Foundations Classroom" and "Time and Space: E-Residencies Offer Flexible Opportunities."

2022

Solo Exhibition: Cacophony

Solo exhibition at the prestigious Czong Institute for Contemporary Art (CICA Museum) in Seoul, South Korea, selected through international juried competition.

2021

International Artist Residencies

Completed two international juried residencies: Politics in Collage Residency at Kolaj Institute in New Orleans and World of Co. Artist Residency in Sofia, Bulgaria.

2019

Regional Arts Commission Grant

Awarded the Regional Arts Commission Artist Support Grant in St. Louis, MO, supporting Robertson's continued artistic development and exhibition work.

2018

Solo Exhibition: Body Politic

Solo exhibition at The Gallery at University City Library in St. Louis, MO, juried by renowned artist and professor Buzz Spector from Washington University.

2017

Solo Exhibition: The Altar of Commerce

Solo exhibition at The Cathy Gregory Gallery in St. Louis, MO, exploring themes of globalization and commercialization in contemporary society.

2016

MFA with Distinction & Multiple Awards

Graduated with an MFA in Painting and Drawing from the University of Washington, receiving the Parnassus Graduating with Excellence Award and Jacob Lawrence Graduate Recognition.
